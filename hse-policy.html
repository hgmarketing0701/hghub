2. Policy Statement

HG Services (M) Sdn Bhd is committed to carrying out all works at the Ombak KLCC Mall pre-opening site without harm to our workers, other contractors' workers on site, mall / developer staff, or anyone else on the premises.

We accept that safety is not separate from the job — it is the job. No deadline, no client request, and no shortcut is worth an injury.

We commit to:

  1. Comply with the Occupational Safety & Health Act 1994 (Act 514), the Factories & Machinery Act 1967, CIDB requirements, and all Ombak KLCC Mall site safety rules.
  2. Identify hazards and control risks before work starts on every shift.
  3. Provide safe tools, equipment, and the correct PPE — and replace anything defective.
  4. Use only trained, briefed, and competent workers on site.
  5. Coordinate with the main contractor / mall management and other trades working in adjacent lots to keep shared access routes safe and clear.
  6. Report, investigate, and learn from every incident and near-miss.
  7. Review this policy regularly and improve it.
Every worker has the right and the duty to STOP WORK if they see an unsafe condition — without fear of blame. Report it to the Site Supervisor immediately.
3. Scope & Application

Ombak KLCC Mall is currently in its pre-opening (fit-out) stage and is not yet open to the public. HG Services is installing hoarding around all lots so that each tenant's appointed contractor can begin renovation works within their own lot.

This policy applies to all HG Services employees, supervisors, sub-contractors, and outsourced workers carrying out work on this site — including hoarding installation, modification, rectification, dismantling, counterweight setup, skirting, and visual print & install.

It applies across all lots, corridors, common areas, and back-of-house zones where our team operates, during all approved working hours, while other contractors are working alongside us on the same pre-opening site.

5. Key Hazards & Controls (Hoarding Works)
HazardControl Measure
Working at height (panel install, top fixing)Use certified ladders / mobile scaffold. No climbing on hoarding. Tools tethered. Trained workers only.
Manual handling (panels, counterweight, doors)Team lift for heavy / long items. Correct lifting posture. Use trolley where possible. No overloading.
Hand & power tools (drills, cutters)Inspect before use. Guards in place. Cut-resistant gloves. No defective tools on site.
Other contractors / trades working in adjacent lotsBarricade the work zone. Warning & overhead-work signage. Coordinate with main contractor. Keep shared corridors & access routes clear.
Slips, trips & falling objectsContinuous housekeeping. No loose materials in walkways. Toe-boards / netting where needed.
Dust & noise (cutting, drilling)Low-dust methods, dust sheeting, vacuum. Schedule noisy work in approved hours. Masks & ear protection.
Electrical (temporary supply)Use mall-approved supply points. RCD/ELCB protection. No damaged cables. Cables routed off walkways.
Fire (hot work, materials)Hot-work permit if required. Fire extinguisher at hot-work area. No blocking fire exits / hose reels / sprinklers.
A full HIRARC (Hazard Identification, Risk Assessment & Risk Control) and Method Statement can be submitted per lot on request before works begin.
6. Safe Work Practices
  1. Daily toolbox briefing before work starts — hazards, scope, PPE, emergency exits.
  2. Permit / approval from Mall HSE obtained before entering or starting work.
  3. Barricade and sign the work zone before any work begins.
  4. PPE worn at all times inside the work area (see Section 7).
  5. No blocking of fire exits, fire-fighting equipment, sprinklers, or escape routes.
  6. Housekeeping throughout the shift and a clean handover at the end.
  7. No horseplay, no alcohol, no drugs on site.
7. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Minimum PPE provided by HG Services and mandatory inside the work area:

  • Safety helmet
  • Safety shoes / boots
  • High-visibility vest
  • Hand gloves (cut-resistant for cutting / handling)
  • Safety glasses & dust mask (cutting / drilling)
  • Ear protection (noisy work)
  • Full body harness (work at height above 2m)
8. Emergency Procedures
  1. On alarm, stop work, make tools safe, and evacuate via the nearest mall exit.
  2. Follow Mall fire wardens and proceed to the designated assembly point.
  3. Supervisor accounts for all HG workers and reports headcount to Mall HSE.
  4. For injury: apply first aid, call mall emergency line, do not move a seriously injured person.
  5. Keep first-aid kit and fire extinguisher accessible at the work area.

9. Incident & Near-Miss Reporting

All incidents, injuries, and near-misses must be reported to the Site Supervisor immediately, and to Mall HSE the same day. HG Services will investigate, record, and apply corrective action to prevent recurrence. Reportable cases are escalated to DOSH as required by law.

10. Training & Competency
  • All workers attend the Mall safety induction before starting.
  • CIDB Green Card held by site workers where required.
  • Workers at height are briefed on harness use and fall prevention.
  • Daily toolbox briefings are recorded with worker sign-off.
11. Environment & Housekeeping
  • Waste and offcuts removed daily — no dumping in mall bins without approval.
  • Dust and noise controlled to protect workers in adjacent lots and meet site rules.
  • No spillage of paint, adhesive, or chemicals onto mall flooring.
  • Work area cleaned and handed over tidy at the end of every shift.
12. Monitoring & Review

This policy is reviewed at least annually, and immediately after any incident or change in scope of work. Compliance is monitored daily by the Site Supervisor and is subject to inspection by Ombak KLCC Mall HSE at any time.
